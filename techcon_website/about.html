Past Speakers:

1. Dr. Anya Sharma

Biography: Dr. Anya Sharma is a leading AI ethicist and the founder of "Algorithmic Justice," a non-profit dedicated to ensuring fairness and transparency in artificial intelligence. With a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Stanford and a background in civil rights advocacy, Dr. Sharma bridges the gap between technological advancement and societal well-being.
Contribution: At TechCon 2022, Dr. Sharma delivered a powerful keynote on "AI for Good: Navigating the Ethical Landscape of Machine Learning." Her insights have significantly influenced policy discussions around responsible AI development and inspired countless developers to integrate ethical considerations into their work.

2. Marcus "Byte" Chen

Biography: Marcus Chen, widely known as "Byte," is a visionary entrepreneur and the co-creator of "QuantumLink," a groundbreaking secure communication protocol that revolutionized data privacy. A self-taught prodigy, he started coding at age 8 and launched his first successful startup by 20.
Contribution: Marcus captivated the TechCon 2023 audience with his session, "The Future of Decentralized Networks," where he unveiled early concepts for a self-sovereign identity platform. His work continues to push the boundaries of cybersecurity and decentralized technologies, making digital interactions safer and more private.

3. Elena Petrova

Biography: Elena Petrova is a renowned environmental data scientist and the architect behind the "EcoGrid" platform, which utilizes satellite imagery and AI to track deforestation and pollution in real-time. Her work provides critical data for conservation efforts and informs global climate policy.
Contribution: At TechCon 2021, Elena presented "Harnessing Big Data for a Greener Planet," showcasing how advanced analytics can be leveraged to address urgent environmental challenges. Her presentation sparked numerous collaborations between tech companies and environmental organizations, demonstrating technology's profound potential for sustainable impact.
